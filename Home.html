What is a CV?

A CV, which stands for curriculum vitae, is a written summary of your work history, education, skills and achievements. Essential to any job application, the aim of a CV is to provide a meaningful summary of your career to date and show prospective employers that you have the necessary skills and competencies to perform well in a job. This means that your CV not only needs to clearly communicate your value but also be correct and free of grammatical and spelling mistakes to have a chance of getting through the first round. Frequently Asked Questions

1. What should I include in my CV?

Include your personal information, work experience, education, skills, and any relevant achievements. Tailor it to the job you're applying for.

2. How long should my CV be?

Generally, keep your CV concise. One to two pages is usually sufficient, but it depends on your experience and the industry.

3. How many pages should it be included?

One or two pages are more efficient for a cv.

4. What is the most applicable color theme for a CV?
